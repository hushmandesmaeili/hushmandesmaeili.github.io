About

History

I am a senior at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) pursuing a B.S./M.S. in Robotics Engineering. I was born and raised in El Salvador from a Persian father and Salvadoran mother. My interests in robotics began in high school when I was first exposed to computer science and object-oriented programming as well as microcontrollers and embedded programming. I further explored my interests in a robotics during a summer program in high school, which confirmed my interests in the field.

In the two years between my high school graduation and arrival at WPI, I worked at the Bahá’í World Centre in Haifa, Israel in the Facility Management Department, where I quickly gained experience in the technical and operational requirements of the engineering work. I led and trained a team of ten facility management personnel from many different countries in a project to upgrade and refurbish the existing lighting fixtures to LED-equivalents.

In the four years that I've been at WPI, I have learned that I really enjoy developing software for robots. I have confirmed that I really enjoy robotics software engineering in the areas of robot kinematics and dynamics, robot controls, manipulation, and motion planning and navigation. I am currently months away from my graduation and I'm OPEN TO WORK and ACTIVELY LOOKING FOR JOBS!

Outside of academics, I am actively engaged in community-building activities. I currently live in Worcester with my wife, and we are constantly engaged in interfaith gatherings in our home, virtues classes for children and an empowerment program for youth. I have been a mentor for the Junior Youth Spiritual Empowerment program, which is a global movement inspiring young people, parents, and community members to work together toward a better world. The program focuses on themes such as cooperation, unity, service to humanity, truthfulness, striving for excellence in their lives and for the community, and justice.

Ultimately, I hope to contribute to the betterment of society and the world through all the aspects of my life, whether as a husband, a friend, a colleague, a volunteer in community-building activities, or as a robotics engineer building robots that can assist and enhance human life.
