Project Description

In a team of 4, we successfully executed a performance of "Romi and Juliet," a modern interpretation of William Shakespeare's play "Romeo and Juliet," using robots and sensor technology. Our custom-built robots were cast as different characters and interacted with each other and their surroundings to perform the scenes. The robots were based on the Pololu 32U4 control board as the microprocessor, running on the Pololu Romi chassis. The AtMega 32U4 microprocessor, LSM6 as the IMU, OpenMV Cam H7 for Aprilag detection, and Arduino Wire library for I2C communication were used in the project.

Contributions

  • Collaborated in team of 4 to build and program robots that autonomously performed scenes from a play.
  • Programmed an AtMega 32U4 microcontroller, its hardware timers/counters and interrupts using C++.
  • Integrated sensors through sensor fusion, including IMU, ultrasonic and IR distance sensors, encoders, and camera.
  • Utilized I2C communication protocol for communication between microcontroller and peripherals.
